<p><p>The debinding furnace market is segmented into low temperature and high temperature furnaces. Low temperature furnaces operate at lower temperatures, typically used for materials sensitive to heat, effectively removing binder materials without affecting the substrate. In contrast, high temperature furnaces function at elevated temperatures, suitable for robust materials that require complete binder removal before sintering. Each type caters to specific industrial applications, ensuring optimal binder elimination while maintaining the integrity and properties of the final components.</p></p>
