<p><p>The canine mammary tumor treatment market encompasses various tumor types, primarily adenomas, carcinomas, and adenocarcinomas. Adenomas are benign tumors that generally have a favorable prognosis with surgical removal. Carcinomas, more aggressive in nature, can invade surrounding tissues and require comprehensive treatment, including surgery and chemotherapy. Adenocarcinomas, a subtype of carcinomas, arise from glandular tissue and are known for their higher malignancy potential, necessitating more intensive therapeutic approaches. Together, these tumor types influence treatment strategies and market demand.</p></p>
